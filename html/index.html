My Favorite European Cars!

Hi! My name is Ethan Kittell I am a car enthusiast and on this webpage I discuss my favorite European cars.

Saab 900/9-3 (Performance variants)

Photo of a 2008 9-3 Turbo X


I am a little biased towards the Saab 9-3 as I do own one. Saab ever since world war 2 has made the world's safest car the Saab 9-3. It was Sweden's iconic sedan/coupe/convertible. While it had more of a luxurious daily driver status Saab did release several "performance" variants of the 9-3 which held their own against Europe's extremely powerful german sports cars.

Car Power Production Numbers Production Years
SPG 160hp Less than 1000 1984-1991
Viggen 230hp About 6000 1998-2001
Turbo X 280hp 2000 2008-2012

The Saab 900/9-3 had a very impressive boost in power over its lifespan. Increasing in 120hp since 1984! The Saab 9-3 also took a very interesting turn over its Production years going from being a dedicated front-wheel drive (FWD) turbo 4-cylinder car to being an all-wheel drive (AWD) turbo 6-cylinder around the mid-2000s. This is very uncommon for sports cars as they tend to stick to a very specific drive train with Saab originally being FWD only. Their sports variants were very limited and loved which makes them excellent sports cars today.


Overall the Saab 900/9-3 had a very impressive run with their sports variants. Sadly Saab as a company no longer exists but their cars are still collected and known around the world. More on their history can be found here.


BMW M3

Photo of a 2015 M3


The m3 has always been the golden standard of a sports car in Europe. BMW made this car not only handle extremely well with its perfectly engineered 50:50 weight distributed chassis but they also made it fast. Every year BMW sought to increase the power on this car's 6 cylinder engine. BMW even introduced for a limited time in 2007 a V8 model of the m3 which was regarded as one of the best V8s ever made by many automotive journalists.

Car Power Production Numbers Production Years
E30 238hp 18,000 1986-1991
E36 270hp 71,000 1992-1999
E46 330hp 86,000 2000-2006
E90 420hp 66,000 2007-2013
F80 425hp 111,000 2014-2020
G80 480hp unknown 2020-present


The BMW m3 made a very impressive power increase throughout its lifespan. Going from 238hp to 480hp this car every year offered a huge power increase (disregarding the F80). In 2014 BMW opted for a twin-turbo 6 cylinder to keep power above 400hp and still maintain a lighter 6-cylinder engine. The older BMWs are some of the most sought-after cars in the automotive world and their newer cars are always certified classics. More history of BMW's M3 models can be found here.


Volkswagen GTI

Photo of a 2001 GTI


The GTI in the 80s was the pioneer of being a people's sports car. Before the GTI if you wanted a performance car you either bought a supercar or you bought a 2 door coupe with a massive engine. But Volkswagen created this "hot hatch" to be a light and affordable option for someone who wants a sports car. Throughout the years the GTI was never known for its amazing performance but more for its iconic styling and feel.


The following table will not feature a "Production Numbers" collumn as it is not a limited production car.

Car Power Production Years
MK1 108hp 1976-1984
MK2 127hp 1987-1992
MK3 148hp 1993-1997
MK4 180hp 1999-2006
MK5/Mk6 200hp 2007-2014
MK7 230hp 2014-2020

The GTI is still being made today with its first models being introduced almost 50 years ago. While its horsepower numbers aren't very high they still make excellent daily drivable sports cars that won't break the bank. GTIs have always kept a clean FWD hatchback design that keeps on being improved. While they aren't particularly rare some collectors will pay high prices for earlier models and some even end up in automotive museums.


The GTI has truly earned the status as the "people's car" and continues to make major engineering breakthroughs with its features. More info on the GTI can be found here.
